1. RULERS & PENS

Step-by-step Solution (English):

  1. Let 'r' be the cost of one ruler, and 'p' be the cost of one pen.
  2. Translate the sentences into equations:
    • $2r + 3p = 13$ (Equation 1)
    • $4r + 5p = 23$ (Equation 2)
  3. Multiply Equation 1 by 2 to make the 'r' coefficient match: $2 \times (2r + 3p = 13) \rightarrow 4r + 6p = 26$ (Equation 3)
  4. Subtract Equation 2 from Equation 3: $(4r + 6p) - (4r + 5p) = 26 - 23 \rightarrow p = 3$. One pen costs 3 dollars.
  5. Substitute p = 3 into Equation 1: $2r + 3(3) = 13 \rightarrow 2r + 9 = 13 \rightarrow 2r = 4 \rightarrow r = 2$. One ruler costs 2 dollars.
  6. Calculate the cost of three rulers and four pens: $3r + 4p = 3(2) + 4(3) = 6 + 12 = 18$.
Answer: 18 dollars.

Langkah-langkah Penyelesaian (Bahasa Indonesia):

    • $4r + 5p = 23$ (Persamaan 2)

  6. Hitung harga tiga penggaris dan empat pulpen: $3r + 4p = 3(2) + 4(3) = 6 + 12 = 18$.

9. BOTTLE POURING

Step-by-step Solution (English) using Working Backwards:

  1. Let the final equal volume in each bottle be 'V'. The total volume of water is $2V$. This total remains constant.
  2. Step 2 (Before B poured into A): B poured $\dfrac{1}{4}$ of its water, leaving $\dfrac{3}{4}$ of it, which became V. So, before this pour, Bottle B had $V \div \dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{4}{3}V$. Bottle A must have had the rest: $2V - \dfrac{4}{3}V = \dfrac{2}{3}V$.
  3. Step 1 (Before A poured into B): A had poured $\dfrac{1}{4}$ of its original water, leaving $\dfrac{3}{4}$ of it, which became $\dfrac{2}{3}V$. So, the initial volume in Bottle A was $\dfrac{2}{3}V \div \dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{8}{9}V$.
  4. The initial volume in Bottle B must be the total volume minus initial A: $2V - \dfrac{8}{9}V = \dfrac{10}{9}V$.
  5. The ratio of initial volume A to initial volume B is $\dfrac{8}{9}V : \dfrac{10}{9}V$.
  6. Simplifying the ratio gives 8:10, which is 4:5.
Answer: 4:5.

Langkah-langkah Penyelesaian (Bahasa Indonesia) dengan Bekerja Mundur:

10. CANDLES

Step-by-step Solution (English):

  1. Let the original length of Candle A be $L_A$ and Candle B be $L_B$.
  2. The rate of burning for A is $\dfrac{L_A}{9}$ per hour. The rate for B is $\dfrac{L_B}{6}$ per hour.
  3. After 2 hours, the length burned for A is $2 \times \dfrac{L_A}{9} = \dfrac{2}{9}L_A$. The remaining height is $L_A - \dfrac{2}{9}L_A = \dfrac{7}{9}L_A$.
  4. After 2 hours, the length burned for B is $2 \times \dfrac{L_B}{6} = \dfrac{1}{3}L_B$. The remaining height is $L_B - \dfrac{1}{3}L_B = \dfrac{2}{3}L_B$.
  5. The heights are the same: $\dfrac{7}{9}L_A = \dfrac{2}{3}L_B$.
  6. To find the ratio $\dfrac{L_A}{L_B}$, we rearrange the equation: $\dfrac{L_A}{L_B} = \dfrac{2}{3} \div \dfrac{7}{9} = \dfrac{2}{3} \times \dfrac{9}{7} = \dfrac{18}{21} = \dfrac{6}{7}$.
Answer: The ratio is 6:7.

Singapore Math Approach: Constant Quantity
This problem is ideal for the "Constant Quantity" or "Unchanged Quantity" method, where we focus on the number of clocks, which does not change.

Step-by-step Solution (English):

  1. Initially: Total items = 1,200. Watches = $60\%$, Clocks = $40\%$.

  7. Number of watches remaining = $20\% \text{ of } 600 = 120$ watches.
  8. Number of watches sold = Initial watches - Final watches = $720 - 120 = 600$.

13. THREE BOYS' MONEY
Singapore Math Approach: Working Backwards
This problem requires us to undo each transaction starting from the end to find the initial amounts.

Step-by-step Solution (English):

  1. Let the final equal amount of money for each boy be $x$.
  2. End: Paul = $x$, Ian = $x$, Calvin = $x$.
  3. Before Ian gave $\dfrac{1}{3}$ to Calvin:
    • Ian was left with $\dfrac{2}{3}$ of his money, which is $x$. So, Ian had $x \div \dfrac{2}{3} = \dfrac{3}{2}x$.
    • Calvin received $\dfrac{1}{3}$ of Ian's money, which is $\dfrac{1}{3} \times \dfrac{3}{2}x = \dfrac{1}{2}x$. So, Calvin started this step with $x - \dfrac{1}{2}x = \dfrac{1}{2}x$.
    • Paul's money was unchanged: $x$.
  4. Before Paul gave 0.3 to Ian:
    • Paul was left with $1 - 0.3 = 0.7$ of his money, which is $x$. So, Paul's initial amount was $x \div 0.7 = \dfrac{x}{7/10} = \dfrac{10}{7}x$.
    • Ian had $\dfrac{3}{2}x$ after receiving money from Paul. The amount he received was $0.3 \times (\text{Paul's initial}) = 0.3 \times \dfrac{10}{7}x = \dfrac{3}{7}x$.
    • So, Ian's initial amount was $\dfrac{3}{2}x - \dfrac{3}{7}x = \dfrac{21x - 6x}{14} = \dfrac{15}{14}x$.
  5. We know Paul and Ian's initial total was 63 dollars.
  6. $(\text{Paul's initial}) + (\text{Ian's initial}) = 63 \rightarrow \dfrac{10}{7}x + \dfrac{15}{14}x = 63$.
  7. $\dfrac{20}{14}x + \dfrac{15}{14}x = 63 \rightarrow \dfrac{35}{14}x = 63 \rightarrow \dfrac{5}{2}x = 63$.
  8. $x = 63 \times \dfrac{2}{5} = 25.2$.
  9. Paul's amount at first was $\dfrac{10}{7}x = \dfrac{10}{7}(25.2) = 10 \times 3.6 = 36$.
Answer: Paul had 36 dollars at first.

Langkah-langkah Penyelesaian (Bahasa Indonesia) dengan Bekerja Mundur:

  1. Misal jumlah uang akhir yang sama untuk setiap anak adalah $x$.
  2. Akhir: Paul = $x$, Ian = $x$, Calvin = $x$.

  9. Substitute UP back into the first equation: $0.95 \times (16500) - CP = 2400$.

  2. Assume all 78 plates were delivered safely.
