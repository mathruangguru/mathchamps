Multiplication and Division: Animal Leg Logic
Code : D4-S320
Part A: Concept Understanding & Visualization
Remember: Chicken/Duck (2 legs), Cow/Cat/Goat (4 legs), Insect/Ant (6 legs), Spider (8 legs).
1. Mr. Budi has 3 cows. He has twice as many chickens as cows.
What is the total number of legs for all the animals?
2. There are 5 cats and 6 ducks in the yard.
What is the difference between the number of cat legs and duck legs?
3. In a mini zoo, there are 4 goats.
How many birds are needed so that the total number of bird legs is equal to the total number of legs of 4 goats?
Answer: birds
4. In Pen A, the total number of animal legs is 24. All animals there are cows, except for 4 chickens.
How many cows are in the pen?
Answer: cows
5. A spider has 8 legs. An ant has 6 legs.

Part B: Mathematical Logic & Problem Solving
6. The teacher divides toy animals into 2 boxes:
  • Box A: 3 Horses
  • Box B: 5 Chickens
Which box has more legs, and what is the difference?
Box has more legs.
7. Animals line up in a repeating pattern:
If this pattern repeats 3 times, what is the total number of legs in the line?
Answer: legs
8. Andi sees a herd of goats. He counts a total of 20 legs.

"Then, 2 goats leave the herd."

How many goat legs are there now?
9. The Farmer (human, 2 legs) is standing next to his 2 buffaloes. Suddenly, 3 geese join them.
Calculate the total legs of all living beings there now!
Answer: legs
10. In a cage, there are Rabbits (4 legs) and Chickens (2 legs). If counted, there are 4 heads and 10 legs in total.
How many rabbits and chickens are there respectively?
Rabbits and Chickens.
