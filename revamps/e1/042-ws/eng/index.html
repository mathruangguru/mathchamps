Learning Guide: Division with Regrouping

When we divide numbers, sometimes we need to regroup a larger place value into a smaller one.

Example: 135 ÷ 3
  • We have 1 Hundred 100. It cannot be divided by 3 as a whole.
  • Regroup 100 into 10 10s.
  • Now there are 13 Tens in total. 13 ÷ 3 is 4 Tens with 1 Ten remainder.
  • Regroup the remaining 1 10 into 10 1s. Total Ones are now 10 + 5 = 15.
  • 15 ÷ 3 = 5. The final result is 45.
