QUANTITY EQUIVALENCE

Calculating Item Values from Relationships of Other Items

Worked Example (Equivalence Method)

Problem: The weight of 3 red boxes is EQUAL TO the weight of 2 blue boxes. If one blue box weighs 15 kg, what is the weight of one red box?

2 Blue Boxes

15

3 Red Boxes
?

Step 1: Find the Total Value.
Total = Number of Items × Unit Value
2 × 15 = 30 kg
(This is the total weight of both sides)
Step 2: Divide into Target Groups.
Total ÷ Number of Red Boxes
30 ÷ 3 = 10 kg
Answer: The weight of one red box is 10 kg.
