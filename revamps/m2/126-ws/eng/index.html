Properties of Isosceles Triangles

Worked Example

Concept: An isosceles triangle has 2 equal sides (marked with tick marks) and 2 equal base angles.
The sum of all angles in a triangle is 180°.

→
Finding the base angle (x):
180° - 40° = 140° (Remaining)
140° ÷ 2 = 70°
x = 70°
Quick Formula: Base Angle = (180° - Vertex Angle) ÷ 2
